What is a Virtual Meeting?

1
Online meeting — people talk and see each other through the internet, not in a real room.
2
Uses video and audio — you can see faces, hear voices, and share ideas in real time.
3
Works from anywhere — join from home, office, school, or any place with internet.
4
Saves time and travel — no need to go to a physical location for every meeting.
5
Used by everyone — students, teachers, office workers, and businesses all use virtual meetings.

Why Virtual Meetings Are Important

Connect Across Distances

Teams and classmates can work together even if they are in different cities or countries.

Save Time and Money

No travel needed. Meetings can happen instantly, saving both time and transport costs.

Flexible Scheduling

Meetings can be arranged quickly. Anyone can join from wherever they are at the right time.

Support Remote Work

Many jobs now allow working from home. Virtual meetings make remote work effective.

What is Google Meet?

1
Google Meet is a free video calling tool made by Google for online meetings.
2
Easy to access — open it from any web browser or download the free app on your phone.
3
Works with Google account — if you have Gmail, you can use Google Meet right away.
4
Supports many people — up to 100 participants can join a free meeting at the same time.
5
Connects with Google tools — works easily with Google Calendar, Gmail, and Google Drive.

Benefits of Google Meet

Free to Use

Anyone with a Google account can start or join a meeting at no cost.

Safe and Secure

Google Meet uses encryption to keep your meetings private and protected.

All Devices

Use it on a computer, tablet, or smartphone — works the same on all.

No Download Needed

Join directly from your browser — no software installation required.

Before Joining a Meeting

1

Check your internet connection. Make sure your WiFi or mobile data is working well.

2

Prepare your device. Charge your phone or laptop so it does not die during the meeting.

3

Test your microphone and camera. Make sure both are working before the meeting starts.

4

Find a quiet place. Choose a location with no loud noises so others can hear you clearly.

5

Open Google Meet early. Join 2–3 minutes before the meeting time so you are ready.

Check Internet Connection

1
Open a website like Google.com to check if your internet is working before the meeting.
2
Use WiFi if possible. WiFi is usually more stable and faster than mobile data for video calls.
3
Stay close to the WiFi router for a stronger signal and fewer connection problems.
4
Close other apps that use the internet. This gives Google Meet more speed and better quality.
5
Restart your router if the connection is slow. This often fixes internet problems quickly.

Prepare Device & Microphone

Charge Your Device

Plug in your laptop or phone before the meeting. A dead battery is very embarrassing.

Test Your Microphone

Go to Google Meet settings and do a microphone test. Make sure your voice is clear.

Test Your Camera

Check that your camera is working and showing a clear image.

Check Your Speaker

Make sure you can hear sound from your device. Adjust the volume before the meeting.

Choose a Quiet Place

1
Find a quiet room where background noise is low. Avoid busy kitchens or noisy streets.
2
Clean your background. A tidy background looks professional. Remove clutter behind you.
3
Use good lighting. Sit facing a window or a lamp so your face is clear and visible.
4
Tell people in your home that you are in a meeting. Ask them to keep noise low during that time.
5
Use a virtual background in Google Meet if your real background is messy or distracting.

Join a Google Meet Meeting

1

Open your browser and go to meet.google.com or open the Google Meet app on your phone.

2

Click the meeting link that was sent to you by email or chat — it will take you directly into the meeting room.

3

Enter the meeting code if you have one. Type the code in the "Enter a code" box and click Join.

4

Allow camera and microphone access when your browser asks. This is needed to participate.

5

Click "Join now" to enter the meeting room. You will see other participants on screen.

Camera On / Off

1
Find the camera button at the bottom of the Google Meet screen — it looks like a small video camera icon.
2
Click to turn off your camera. A red line will appear on the icon to show that your camera is off.
3
Click again to turn on. The icon becomes white again when your camera is on and showing your face.
4
Keep camera on when speaking or presenting. It helps others see you and feel connected to you.
5
Turn camera off if your background is very distracting or if your internet is slow.

Mute & Unmute Microphone

✅ Good Habits ❌ Avoid These
🎤 Mute yourself when you are not speaking to reduce background noise for others. 🚫 Do not leave your mic on with loud background sounds like TV, traffic, or dogs barking.
🔊 Unmute only when it is your turn to speak or when asked a question. 🚫 Do not forget to unmute before speaking — others will not hear you at all.
⌨️ Use keyboard shortcut Ctrl + D (or Cmd + D on Mac) to quickly mute / unmute. 🚫 Do not talk over other people. Wait for them to finish speaking first.
👂 Always listen for your name before unmuting and speaking in a large meeting. 🚫 Do not eat food near your microphone — the sound is very distracting for others.

Use the Chat Box

1
Open the chat by clicking the speech bubble icon at the bottom right of Google Meet.
2
Type your question without interrupting the speaker. The host will read and answer it when ready.
3
Share links or important information in the chat so everyone can access it easily.
4
Say hello or react in the chat — it is polite to greet others when you first join the meeting.
5
Keep messages professional. Use clear and respectful language in all chat messages.

Share Screen

1

Click "Present now" at the bottom of the screen.

2

Choose sharing type: Your whole screen, a window, or a tab.

3

Select the content and click the "Share" button to start.

4

Look for the red border showing that your screen is being shared.

5

Click "Stop presenting" when you are finished sharing.

Privacy Tip: Close any private tabs, emails, or messages before you start sharing your screen.

Meeting Etiquette Overview

Greet Politely

Say hello when you join and goodbye when you leave. Small greetings make a good impression.

Listen Actively

Pay attention to the speaker. Do not browse other websites during the meeting.

Silence Your Phone

Turn off notifications and phone ringtones before joining. Sounds are distracting.

Wait Your Turn

Do not interrupt others. Raise your hand or use the chat to let others know you want to speak.

Be On Time

1
Join 2–3 minutes early. Being early shows you are prepared and respectful of others' time.
2
Check the meeting time again before you join. Sometimes time zones are different for international meetings.
3
Set a reminder on your phone or computer 10 minutes before the meeting is scheduled to start.
4
If you are late, join quietly, mute your mic first, and do not interrupt the meeting already in progress.
5
Tell the host in advance if you know you will be late or cannot attend. Always be considerate.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Forgetting to unmute before speakingAlways check that your microphone is ON before you start talking.
Poor internet connection during the meetingTest your connection before the meeting. Switch to WiFi if mobile data is weak.
Bad lighting — face is too dark or too brightSit facing a light source. Avoid sitting with a bright window directly behind you.
Checking your phone while the meeting is activeThis is disrespectful. Put your phone away or on silent mode.
Talking over other people or interruptingWait for the speaker to finish. Use the chat or raise-hand feature to get attention.

Conclusion

1
Prepare well before every meeting — test your internet, camera, microphone, and choose a quiet place.
2
Use Google Meet features correctly — camera, mute button, chat box, and screen sharing all help you communicate better.
3
Always be on time and be respectful — good etiquette makes meetings productive and shows your professionalism.
4
Avoid common mistakes — mute when not speaking, fix your lighting, and stay focused during the meeting.
5
Practice makes perfect — the more you join virtual meetings, the more confident and skilled you will become.
